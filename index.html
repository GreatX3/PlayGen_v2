Multi-Granularity Editing

Video editing is an important application of video generation. In interactive video generation, it is possible to edit not only the video content (e.g., changing an object's color and texture) like video editing, but also the interactive mechanics (e.g., adding an interactable object). Learning both editing in a single model has high complexity. A better way is to decouple the two types of editing and learn them separately. Hence, we propose a hybird model consisting of an interactive mechanics simulator and a visual renderer to learn interactive mechanics editing and video content editing, respectively. The interactive mechanics simulator is built on Yan-Sim, levearging its strong simulation of complex interaction mechanics. And the visual renderer takes advantage of Yan-Gen for its powerful generation capabilities on various open-domain video content. We use the depth map as intermediate state to connect these two mudules. To facilitate convenient and controllable editing, we employ two types of text prompts: structure prompt for interactive mechanics editing (also called structure editing) and style prompt for visual content editing (also called style editing). With this hybird model, it is able to achieve multi-granularity editing through text during interaction.
